Terence the Salamander

The Terence the Salamander sticker series showcases the fusion of design and technology, creating interactive, scannable stickers. This project highlights the potential of programmable graphics for marketing, authentication, and digital engagement.

Project Brief

The Terence experiment showcases my ability to conceptualize, design, and bring a product to market, blending creativity with technical precision. This programmable sticker, inspired by the salamander’s symbolism of adaptability and transformation, was developed to demonstrate how scannable technology can elevate user engagement and provide practical applications for marketing, product authentication, and events.

Executive Summary

This experiment involved the end-to-end development of a programmable sticker, including designing and prototyping with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator, crafting a compelling product story, creating and producing custom packaging, reaching out to retail partners for market viability, optimizing production costs for scalability, and testing live prototypes to gather user feedback.

Objectives

  • Develop a programmable sticker using Spot Vision’s encoded graphic technology for interactive applications.
  • Ensure precision and high-fidelity production through vector design, registration marks, and accurate cutting.
  • Optimize scalability with cost-effective production methods and explore retail partnerships.
  • Enhance user engagement by testing prototypes and refining interactivity for real-world use.

Design Approach

The “Terence the Salamander” sticker series was designed and produced using Spot Vision’s encoded graphic technology, ensuring each sticker featured unique, programmable codes. Custom registration marks in Adobe Acrobat enabled precise alignment for cutting and lamination, while a cloud-based system dynamically refreshed encoded graphics. High-fidelity production was maintained through Cricut precision cutting and lamination, ensuring durability and scannability. This process created interactive, functional stickers ready for integration into digital ecosystems, blending art, technology, and automation.

Conclusion

The Terence experiment is a successful blend of creative vision and technical execution, serving as a model for how programmable visuals can redefine engagement in modern marketing and design practices.
