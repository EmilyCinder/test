TERENCE THE SALAMANDER

ENCODING DATA INTO GRAPHIC STICKERS

PROJECT OVERVIEW

THE TERENCE THE SALAMANDER STICKER SERIES SHOWCASES THE FUSION OF DESIGN AND TECHNOLOGY, CREATING INTERACTIVE, SCANNABLE STICKERS. THIS PROJECT DEMONSTRATES HOW PROGRAMMABLE GRAPHICS CAN SOLVE REAL-WORLD PROBLEMS IN MARKETING, PRODUCT AUTHENTICATION, AND DIGITAL ENGAGEMENT THROUGH INNOVATIVE STICKER TECHNOLOGY.

THE PROBLEM

TRADITIONAL STICKERS SERVE LIMITED PURPOSES - DECORATION OR BASIC INFORMATION. THERE'S GROWING NEED FOR PHYSICAL PRODUCTS THAT BRIDGE THE DIGITAL WORLD, OFFERING INTERACTIVE EXPERIENCES AND AUTHENTICATION CAPABILITIES.

THE SOLUTION

PROGRAMMABLE STICKERS WITH ENCODED DATA THAT CAN BE SCANNED TO UNLOCK DIGITAL CONTENT OR VERIFY AUTHENTICITY - CREATING A SEAMLESS PHYGITAL (PHYSICAL+DIGITAL) EXPERIENCE.

PROJECT BRIEF

EXPERIMENT

THE TERENCE EXPERIMENT SHOWCASES MY ABILITY TO CONCEPTUALIZE, DESIGN, AND BRING A PRODUCT TO MARKET, BLENDING CREATIVITY WITH TECHNICAL PRECISION. THIS PROGRAMMABLE STICKER WAS DEVELOPED TO DEMONSTRATE HOW SCANNABLE TECHNOLOGY CAN ELEVATE USER ENGAGEMENT.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

THIS EXPERIMENT INVOLVED THE END-TO-END DEVELOPMENT OF A PROGRAMMABLE STICKER, INCLUDING DESIGNING AND PROTOTYPING WITH ADOBE CREATIVE SUITE, CREATING CUSTOM PACKAGING, EXPLORING RETAIL PARTNERSHIPS, OPTIMIZING PRODUCTION COSTS, AND TESTING PROTOTYPES.

OBJECTIVES

DEVELOP A PROGRAMMABLE STICKER USING ENCODED GRAPHIC TECHNOLOGY
ENSURE PRECISION THROUGH VECTOR DESIGN AND ACCURATE PRODUCTION
OPTIMIZE PRODUCTION METHODS FOR SCALABILITY
ENHANCE USER ENGAGEMENT THROUGH INTERACTIVE PROTOTYPES

DESIGN APPROACH

THE "TERENCE THE SALAMANDER" STICKER SERIES WAS DESIGNED USING ENCODED GRAPHIC TECHNOLOGY, ENSURING EACH STICKER FEATURED UNIQUE, PROGRAMMABLE CODES. CUSTOM REGISTRATION MARKS ENABLED PRECISE ALIGNMENT FOR CUTTING AND LAMINATION.

HIGH-FIDELITY PRODUCTION WAS MAINTAINED THROUGH PRECISION CUTTING TECHNIQUES, ENSURING DURABILITY AND SCANNABILITY. THIS CREATED FUNCTIONAL STICKERS READY FOR INTEGRATION INTO DIGITAL ECOSYSTEMS, BLENDING ART AND TECHNOLOGY.

PROCESS GALLERY

CONCLUSION

THE TERENCE EXPERIMENT SUCCESSFULLY CREATED A BRIDGE BETWEEN PHYSICAL PRODUCTS AND DIGITAL EXPERIENCES. THE PROJECT DEMONSTRATED THAT PROGRAMMABLE STICKERS CAN EFFECTIVELY SERVE MULTIPLE FUNCTIONS:

  • AUTHENTICATION MARKERS FOR PRODUCT VERIFICATION
  • INTERACTIVE ELEMENTS THAT CONNECT TO DIGITAL CONTENT
  • COST-EFFECTIVE PRODUCTION AT SCALE
  • RETAIL-READY PACKAGING AND PRESENTATION

THIS SOLUTION REPRESENTS A MODEL FOR HOW PROGRAMMABLE VISUALS CAN ENHANCE BOTH CREATIVE DESIGN AND TECHNICAL APPLICATIONS IN PHYSICAL PRODUCTS.
