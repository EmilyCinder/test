Brand Integration: Encoded Logo Experiment

This experiment focused on layering branded logos on top of encoded graphic patterns to test their impact on scannability and overall visual cohesion — specifically used to prototype a custom shirt for Isai.

Executive Summary

We explored how encoded patterns can be blended with company branding to produce functional, scannable graphics while maintaining visual fidelity. The experiment tested whether layering SVG logo artwork onto encoded graphics would interfere with scannability or strengthen it as a design system — particularly for merchandise, partnerships, and authentication.

Introduction

The idea was to test a use case for fashion and brand collaboration — combining a company’s logo with our encoded graphics as a seamless overlay. This was particularly interesting for product authentication and co-branded marketing scenarios. Isai’s custom shirt became the prototype for this application.

Objectives

  • Test whether logos layered over encoded graphics remain scannable.
  • Prototype collaborative merch combining SVG logos and encoded patterns.
  • Explore applications for brand authentication and unique collectibles.
  • Evaluate visibility, aesthetics, and technical constraints for overlay designs.

Design Process

Using Photoshop, I adjusted and layered an SVG logo directly on top of a previously encoded red pattern. Visual blending and logo visibility were refined to preserve the encoded structure underneath. This required working carefully with opacity, color contrast, and vector blending modes to maintain scannability.

The result was a unique t-shirt design worn and scanned in real-world environments. The success of the Isai shirt proved this layering concept works, and can extend the functionality of encoded media into collaborative visual identity systems.

Conclusion

The Brand Integration experiment showed that layering logos on top of encoded patterns can be successful when executed with technical consideration. It opens the door for future partnerships where brand identity can merge with encrypted digital access — expanding the creative and commercial potential of Spot Vision’s technology.
