Solving Brand Integration with Encoded Logos

This experiment explored how to integrate company branding with encoded graphics — specifically, whether logos could be layered over scannable patterns without disrupting their function. We tested this in a real-world context by designing a custom shirt for Isai that combines logo visibility with scan performance.

The Problem

As fashion merges with digital tools, the challenge is how to maintain branding while enabling interactive, scannable features. Traditional logos can clash visually or disrupt encoded structures. We needed to determine whether layered branding could enhance or harm scannability — especially for authentication and co-branded merchandise.

The Solution

We developed a method for layering SVG logos on top of encoded graphics using vector editing tools. This aimed to create a hybrid visual language that preserved scannability while introducing brand identity. The Isai prototype shirt served as the testing ground for this design strategy.

Problem-Solving Approach

  • Created encoded graphics as a base layer using Spot Vision’s design pipeline.
  • Overlaid branded SVG logos on top, adjusting opacity, blend modes, and scale.
  • Tested visual legibility and scannability using phones and computer vision tools.
  • Refined mockups to find a balance between aesthetic and technical clarity.

Design Execution

The final composition was assembled in Photoshop, where we layered the logo over a red encoded pattern and adjusted contrast for clarity. We printed the graphic onto a t-shirt, ensuring the encoding was still functional in both indoor and outdoor environments. Isai wore the shirt for real-world testing.

Outcomes

The resulting prototype confirmed that branded overlays can be applied without disrupting scannability — as long as spacing, contrast, and logo placement are technically considered. This opens up future potential for fashion-tech collabs, co-branded drops, and authentication layers that are both expressive and functional.
